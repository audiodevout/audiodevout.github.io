Atharva Gupta

Artist Identity

I’m Atharva Gupta, known as asymmetrica, an interdisciplinary artist originally from Varanasi and Ajmer, India, now based in Groningen, Netherlands. With a background spanning Psychology, Sociology, Literature, and contemporary media arts, I blend academic rigor with hands-on creative coding and kinetic sound installation practices.

Practice Overview

My work operates at the intersection of sound, light, code, and physical structures. I explore the emotional and sensory dimensions of technology — how machines evoke feelings and blur boundaries between control and chaos. Through generative and interactive systems, I create immersive audiovisual environments, often reflecting on urban entropy, affective noise, and the poetics of imperfection.

Key Projects

  • Rain Reminders: A 1.8m tall motorized rain stick sculpture that meditates on tinnitus and the dialogue between organic and industrial sound.
  • Symmetrical Fictions: Video installations derived from gameplay data, translating urban garbage truck paths into sonic and visual narratives.
  • Fever Dream: Audio-reactive poetry installations confronting fear and abstraction through immersive, glitchy soundscapes.

Theoretical Foundations

My practice is informed by thinkers such as Michel Foucault, Jürgen Habermas, and Jacques Rancière, alongside feminist and posthumanist perspectives from Donna Haraway and Claire Bishop. I engage with ideas around alterity, arts cognition (notably by Barend van Heusden), and arts sociology through the lens of Roland Barthes, Gilles Deleuze, and Félix Guattari. This framework helps me navigate the politics and poetics of sensing, presence, and technological mediation.

Inspirations

I draw creative energy from diverse sources spanning kinetic and sound artists like Trimpin, Studio Zimoun, and Reuben Margolin, to generative and data-driven visionaries such as Ryoji Ikeda, Memo Akten, and Rafael Lozano-Hemmer. Conceptual provocateurs like Hito Steyerl and Ai Weiwei inspire critical reflections in my work, while the aesthetics of Christina Kubisch and Daito Manabe inform my approach to sound and technology.

Literary & Cultural Influences

My literary tastes range from the playful nonsense of Edward Lear to the philosophical depth of Ralph Waldo Emerson, and the raw honesty of Charles Bukowski. I’m deeply influenced by Hindu mythology and its cinematic retellings, alongside the adventurous spirit of comics like Asterix, Tintin, and Chacha Chowdhary. A lifelong consumer of mainstream media and superhero narratives, I embrace the power of popular storytelling in shaping cultural imagination.

Tools & Methods

My creative toolkit includes TouchDesigner (both as a tool and teaching platform), Ableton Live, Arduino, and sensor-driven systems. I blend generative coding, physical computing, and interactive audiovisual design to craft environments where sound, light, and movement coalesce.
